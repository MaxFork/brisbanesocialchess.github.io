Brisbane Social Chess Inc – Rules

Brisbane Social Chess Inc IA4682418 ABN 20129327738

  1. The name of the incorporated association is Brisbane Social Chess Inc. (‘the club’).
  2. The objects of the club are to:
    • Promote the playing of chess in person in amenable surroundings in and around the City of Brisbane in Queensland Australia.
    • Be the cause of dormant chess players to take up the game again.
    • Using social enterprise principles, encourage fun, free and friendly social chess everywhere.
    • Welcome and encourage everyone.
    • Promote chess as a constructive pastime for adults and children to learn.
    • Spark resilience and develop mental toughness in response to loss and defeat.
    • Protect and foster universal values of competition, merit, equal opportunity, and freedom.
  3. The club has all the powers of an individual.
  4. The club will take control over the present unincorporated association known as the Brisbane Social Chess group on Meetup.com and the Facebook Page Brisbane Social Chess.
  5. The membership of the club and subclasses of members is unlimited.
  6. All members are collectively referred to as ‘Members’ but Members who are not Financial Members are referred to as ‘Social Members’.
  7. All Members are categorised into one (or more) of the following subclasses:
    • Unpaid: never paid a membership fee or donated.
    • Paid: paid at least once, but lapsed.
    • Financial: paid and active — eligible to vote and join committee.
    • Sponsor: donated to the club.
  8. Applications and payments must be made online.
  9. Default membership fees (unless changed):
    • Joining fee: $10
    • Annual membership: $10
  10. The committee may accept/reject members or delegate this to a person or automated system.
  11. Members may resign anytime via written notice.
  12. The committee may expel members in the best interest of the club.
  13. Appeals must be lodged in writing within 28 days and will be heard by the committee.
  14. A member register will be maintained with name, email, and payment details.
  15. The secretary must be a Financial Member living in or near Brisbane.
  16. Vacancies for secretary must be filled within 14 days.
  17. The secretary is part of the committee.
  18. The committee may remove the secretary.
  19. The committee includes president, treasurer, secretary, and optionally others.
  20. All committee members must retire at each AGM but can be re-elected.
  21. Committee members may resign any time via written notice.
  22. They may also be removed by 2/3 vote at a general meeting — no appeal allowed.
  23. The committee manages club affairs and finances. The secretary handles security items.
  24. Committee meetings can be virtual and must occur at least every 3 months. Quorum: 3.
  25. First AGM must be held within 3 months after the first financial year ends.
  26. Subsequent AGMs must occur yearly within 3 months of end of financial year.
  27. AGM business includes minutes, financial report, elections, and appointing auditor.
  28. General meetings can be called by the president, secretary, committee majority or 25 Financial Members.
  29. At least 14 days' notice is required for general meetings via email.
  30. General meeting quorum is 7 or all Financial Members (whichever is less).
  31. If no quorum, reschedule to same time next week — those attending form quorum.
  32. Meetings may be held with approved technology (e.g. Zoom).
  33. General resolutions decided by majority of Financial Members present.
  34. No proxies allowed. Voting method decided by committee.
  35. Minutes of meetings must be recorded and available to members upon request.
  36. By-laws may be created by committee and amended by special resolution.
  37. Rule changes require 75% vote at general meeting and must be registered to take effect.
  38. Club funds must be in bank accounts requiring two signatures and committee approval.
  39. Proper financial records must be maintained and reviewed regularly.
  40. Treasurer must prepare a financial statement after each financial year.
  41. Club income and property must only support its objects.
  42. Financial year ends on 30 June.
  43. If wound up, surplus assets must go to another similar non-profit entity.
